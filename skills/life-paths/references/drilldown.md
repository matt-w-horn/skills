# Stage 3: Drill-down

Goal: resolve the specific unknowns and contradictions that would change which paths get drawn or how they are judged. This stage is targeted, not exhaustive; its filter is materiality. Before asking anything, ask yourself: would either possible answer change a path, a constraint, or the recommendation? If not, log the uncertainty and move on. People have finite patience, and spending it here means less of it at the stages that need them.

## What to hunt

Work through the dossier and evidence log looking for four kinds of tension:

1. **Stated versus revealed preference.** They say they want X; the record shows a decade of steps toward Y and none toward X. Or the inverse and more hopeful case: an interest they mention offhand that the record shows them pursuing unprompted, repeatedly, across contexts. The second kind is often the most valuable finding in the whole process, especially for a person who told you their introspection is unreliable, because a stated interest that matches years of unpaid behavior has double confirmation.
2. **Self-assessment versus the record.** They call themselves bad at something their reviews repeatedly praise, or the reverse. Both directions matter: undersold strengths expand the path space; unexamined weaknesses put risk in it.
3. **Plan versus behavior.** The financial plan says one thing, the spending record another; the stated priority is family time, the calendar says seventy-hour weeks. Whatever the pair, the tension is the data.
4. **Plain gaps.** Territories from intake that stayed thin, missing years, a role they skated past. Skating is sometimes an accident and sometimes a signal; a gentle "you moved quickly past X, anything there that matters for this?" is enough, and take a deflection as an answer.

## How to raise a contradiction

Present both pieces of evidence neutrally and ask which is right, without a gotcha and without a theory attached: "You said X. The record shows Y and Y again. Help me reconcile these." Then listen, because all resolutions are useful and they differ: sometimes the stated preference is aspirational and they know it; sometimes the record was constrained by circumstances that no longer hold; sometimes they had never noticed and the noticing is itself worth the whole interview. Record the resolution and its evidence tag. Never present a contradiction as a verdict on their self-knowledge; the tone is two facts on the table between you, not a cross-examination.

## Revealed-preference probes

For the person whose introspection is unreliable (per intake's calibration), or wherever stated evidence is thin, use behavioral probes:

- **The finished/abandoned list.** Everything substantial they started in the last five years, sorted into finished and abandoned. The sort itself, and their commentary while sorting, maps what actually holds their attention.
- **The return test.** After something shipped, what did they voluntarily go back to? Post-completion return is one of the cleanest enjoyment signals available.
- **The calendar audit.** Where discretionary hours actually went in a recent representative month. The calendar has no narrative to defend.
- **The observer relay.** If a trusted observer was offered in intake, use them now: "from the outside, what work makes them come alive, and what work visibly drains them?" Relay through the person; their reaction to the observer's answer is a second data point.
- **The unprompted-build inventory.** Things made that nobody asked for. For many people this list is short and extremely concentrated, and its concentration points somewhere.

## Closing the stage

Update `dossier.md` and `evidence.md` with resolutions, and add a short "weights" note stating, explicitly, how stated versus behavioral evidence should be weighted for this person and why; the analysis and path agents read it. The stage ends when remaining unknowns are immaterial, and it is fine, normal, and worth saying to the person, for a few tensions to close as "unresolved, carried as uncertainty" rather than forced to a tidy answer.
